# How to Verify a Product — Consumer Guide

This guide explains how to check whether a product is genuine and trace its journey from origin to the shelf. No account or technical knowledge required.

---

## What is Supply-Link?

Supply-Link lets producers record every step of a product's life — from harvest to retail — on a public, tamper-proof ledger. As a consumer, you can read that record at any time to confirm a product is authentic and see exactly where it has been.

---

## Step 1 — Find the QR Code on the Product

Look for a QR code on the product's label, packaging, or receipt. It usually appears alongside text like "Verify authenticity" or "Scan to trace."

---

## Step 2 — Scan or Enter the Product ID

You have two options:

### Option A — Scan with your phone camera

1. Go to [supply-link.app](https://supply-link.app) (or open the site on your phone).
2. In the **"Verify a Product"** section on the home page, tap **Scan QR**.
3. Allow camera access when your browser asks.
4. Point your camera at the QR code on the product. The app reads it automatically — no button press needed.

> **Camera not working?** Some browsers block camera access by default. Check your browser settings and make sure the site has permission to use the camera. You can also use Option B instead.

### Option B — Type the product ID manually

1. Find the product ID printed near the QR code (it looks like `prod-001` or a longer code).
2. Type or paste it into the **"Enter product ID"** field on the home page.
3. Press **Verify**.

---

## Step 3 — Read the Product Journey

After scanning or entering the ID, you will see the product's full history as a timeline. Each entry shows:

| What you see | What it means |
|---|---|
| **Harvest** 🌱 | The product was collected or produced at this location and date. |
| **Processing** ⚙️ | The product was cleaned, sorted, or transformed at this facility. |
| **Shipping** 🚢 | The product left this location and was transported. |
| **Retail** 🏪 | The product arrived at a store or distribution point. |

Each step also shows the date and time it was recorded, and the location where it happened.

---

## Step 4 — Check the "Verified on Stellar" Badge

At the top of the product page you will see a badge that reads **"Verified on Stellar · View Contract"**.

- This badge means the product's history is stored on the Stellar blockchain — a public record that nobody can secretly change or delete.
- Clicking the badge opens the Stellar Explorer, where you can see the raw on-chain record if you want to dig deeper.

**If the badge is missing or the page shows "Product Not Found"**, the product ID does not match any registered product. This could mean:
- The QR code is damaged or counterfeit.
- The product was not registered with Supply-Link.
- You may have mistyped the product ID — try scanning the QR code instead.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Do I need an account to verify a product?**
No. Verification is completely open. Anyone can check any product at any time.

**Do I need a crypto wallet?**
No. Wallets are only needed by producers and logistics partners who add information to the record. As a consumer, you just read it.

**Is my scan tracked or stored?**
No personal data is collected when you verify a product. The verification page is read-only.

**What if the timeline only shows one or two steps?**
Not every product goes through all four stages. A locally produced item might only have a Harvest and Retail entry. Fewer steps are not a sign of a problem — what matters is that the steps shown are genuine.

**Can the history be faked?**
Each entry is written to the Stellar blockchain by an authorized party and cannot be altered after the fact. Only actors that the product owner has explicitly approved can add events. If an entry appears, it was genuinely recorded by someone in the supply chain.
